M SAIFULLAH SAIF

Social Worker & Youth Organizer

A climate warrior, who speaks at various forums on various issues and prospects of sub-tropical regions of Bangladesh. He has implemented several projects in subtropical regions through his organization with the support of UNICEF to combat the effects of climate change. He is making a special contribution to social development by establishing youth organizations.

Biography

M Saifullah Saif is a Bangladeshi youth organizer. As a student, he enlightened himself through various social development activities. He was born on March 1, 1995 in a noble Muslim family at Kuakata in Patuakhali. He is the youngest among six brothers and two sisters. He grew up in a family and received his primary education. He completed his graduation in Economics from Barguna Government College under National University. Currently, he is doing post graduation in Govt BM College, Barisal. Apart from this, he is currently studying law and pursuing Diploma in ICT from Sheikh Hasina National Institute of Youth Development.

His father used to help the local citizens to deal with various natural calamities. These social activities of his father inspired him in his childhood. He extended a helping hand to the local lower middle class people to grow up with the family. As a child, his philanthropic attitude made him popular among the people of the costal area.

During his graduation, he formed a youth organization called Barguna Youth Forum with his classmates. At the initial stage there were only seven members. Later there were more than 200 members who raised funds for the needy and helpless people. From that fund they provided winter clothes to more than eight thousand people. They provide financial assistance to students, conduct various training programs to develop youth leadership. On completion of his graduation, he left Barguna and resigned from the organization. Besides, he was a volunteer of Bangladesh Red Crescent Society, Barguna Unit.

He later joined Kuakata Tarun Club, a popular youth organization in his hometown. Where the people have to live by constantly facing the storm. Kuakata Tarun Club helps people in these calamities. Within a few days, Mr. Saif became the spokesperson of his organization. He later worked on climate resilient health care through an agreement his organization had with UNICEF. Today he is known in his community as a climate warrior.
